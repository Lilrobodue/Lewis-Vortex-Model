What this is

A CLI-driven forward model that computes planet positions from disk physics, replacing v1’s 30 per-planet dials with ten literature-motivated global parameters. Boundaries (dead-zone edges, snow line) are derived; positions are outputs; every position-determining event emits a structured mechanism-log record. Validation ships null models and Monte-Carlo chance rates computed before scoring, so the instrument can — and does — fail.

The project’s honest verdict, in one line: the physics captures the deterministic geometric organization of planetary systems (Hill-regulated “peas-in-a-pod” spacing) near the achievable limit, but adds no independent information about planet demographics. It earns a real, falsifiable spacing leg — not a demographics one.

The result, honestly

  • The first held-out pass looked like a decisive 1/313 failure — traced to unfair scoring against transit-truncated data and parameters borrowed from the Sun, not to the physics.
  • Scored fairly and with the ten globals solved (not guessed), the model beats a geometric-regularity null ~22% of held-out systems — pre-registered and confirmed.
  • That null is not a strawman: it is mutual-Hill packing (K ≈ 20; mass-uniformity ↔ spacing-uniformity, p = 10⁻¹⁰). The model reproduces it, so ~22% sits near a real predictability ceiling.
  • Its one un-ceilinged prediction — giant occurrence — was tested to conclusion and fails: no information beyond stellar metallicity + mass.
